Hoy. My name is Matěj Grabovský and I am a self-proclaimed web designer and general-purpose coder living in a small town in Central Europe. I am young, friendly and able to grasp new concepts quickly. My working style and ethos are influenced by minimalism and focus on function, semantics and aesthetics.

I have six years of experince in website development, both on the front and back end. I am comfortable with modern technologies, such as HTML5, CSS3, and many of the contemporary JavaScript libraries (primarily jQuery) as much as with vanilla JavaScript. Furthermore, I like to sport these technologies whenever it is possible.

On the server side, I am used to running Apache HTTPD or nginx on Unix-based systems. I oft use PHP, although I do not fear saner languages, such as Python, Ruby or JavaScript. For data storage, I employ relational databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL and SQLite) and “NoSQL” databases (specifically MongoDB; alternatively Redis and memcached for fast and key–value storage).

I have very good knowledge of PHP and JavaScript, decent knowledge of C and Python, and mild experience with C++. I focus mainly on web and command line interfaces. However, I have experience creating GUI applications with the Qt and wxPython.

I am familiar with code review process and version control systems, namely Subversion and Git.

You can get to know some of my code on GitHub, thoughts, opinions and interests on Twitter, musical tastes on Last.fm, and photography skills on Flickr. Occasionaly, I answer questions on Stack Overflow.

My editor of choice is vim, colorscheme solarized or base16-based, I prefer UTF-8, tabs for indentation, one space after full stop, and UK spelling. I am seldom confused about placement of the comma and articles.

I have a fairly wide range of interests: among others, mathematics, physics, linguistics, literature, typography, photography and jogging.
