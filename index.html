Hoy. My name is Matěj Grabovský and I am a self-proclaimed coder, website designer and general-purpose developer living in a small town in the Czech Republic. I am young, friendly, responsible, flexible and able to grasp new, uknown concepts quickly. My working style and ethos are influenced by minimalism and focus on function, semantics, aesthetics and detail.

I have five years of experince in website development, both on the front and back end. I am comfortable with modern technologies, such as HTML5, CSS3, and many of the fancy JavaScript libraries (including jQuery, Backbone.js, and Underscore.js) as much as with vanilla JavaScript/CoffeeScript. Furthermore, I like to sport these technologies whenever I can.

On the server side, I am used to running Apache HTTPD or nginx servers on Linux. I oft use PHP, although I do not fear saner languages, such as Python, Ruby or JavaScript, either. For data storage, I employ relational databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL and SQLite) and “NoSQL” databases (specifically, MongoDB; eventually Redis and memcached for key–value storage, prevalently for caching).

I have very good knowledge of PHP and JavaScript, decent knowledge of C and Python, and mild experience with C++. I focus mainly on web and command line interfaces; my favourite all-purpose C++ library is dlib. However, I have used the Qt and wxPython frameworks to build GUI applications on Windows.

I am familiar with code review process and version control systems, namely, Subversion and Git.

You can find some of my code on GitHub, thoughts, opinions and interests on Twitter, musical tastes on Last.fm, and photography on Flickr. Now and then, I answer questions on Stack Overflow.

My editor of choice is vim, colorscheme solarized, mustang or molokai. I prefer UTF-8, tabs for indentation (unless rules require otherwise), one space after full stop, and UK spelling and pronunciation.

I have a fairly wide range of interests: among others, mathematics, physics, literature, typography, photography, hiking and running.
